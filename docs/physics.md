### Vehicle dynamics and physical loading principles

Research how vehicle motion affects cargo positioning, weight distribution and securing.

Explain:

* Whether trucks should normally be loaded from the front wall near the cab towards the rear, and when this rule does not apply
* The difference between positioning cargo against the front wall for blocking and positioning cargo to achieve legal axle loads
* How emergency braking creates forward cargo forces
* How acceleration creates rearward forces
* How cornering creates lateral forces
* How road bumps and vertical acceleration affect stacking, tipping and securing
* Why cargo can continue moving due to inertia even after the vehicle begins braking
* Why an empty gap in front of unsecured cargo increases the impact force on the front wall
* Why heavy cargo should generally be positioned low and close to the vehicle’s longitudinal centreline
* How a high centre of gravity affects rollover and cargo tipping
* How longitudinal cargo position changes front-axle, rear-axle, kingpin and trailer-axle loads
* How lateral cargo position changes left-right wheel and suspension loading
* How vehicle handling may be affected by insufficient steering-axle or drive-axle load
* How load distribution changes after each delivery stop
* Whether remaining cargo can become unsafe after earlier cargo is unloaded

Include simplified static-equilibrium formulas for rigid trucks and semi-trailers, worked examples, and text-described diagrams.

Clearly distinguish:

1. Cargo positioning for axle-weight distribution
2. Cargo positioning for stability and centre of gravity
3. Cargo positioning for loading and unloading accessibility
4. Cargo securing against acceleration forces

Do not assume that cargo should always be placed near the cab. Determine the correct position from axle limits, vehicle geometry, front-wall strength, centre of gravity, delivery sequence and securing requirements.

For the application, recommend:

* Hard rejection when an axle or gross-weight limit is exceeded
* Continuous front/rear and left/right balance indicators
* Visual centre-of-gravity display
* Forward, rearward and lateral securing-force estimates
* Warnings when cargo relies on an uncertified front wall
* Recalculation of axle loads after every delivery stop
* Detection of configurations that are initially legal but become unsafe after partial unloading
* Clear separation between estimated generic calculations and calculations using manufacturer-specific vehicle data
