The problem

Anyone can check if it fits. The hard part is everything after.

Warehouse and transport teams plan loads for fit. Then reality hits the road: the first delivery is buried behind the last, an axle is over its legal limit, the load is rear-heavy at 90 km/h.

A plan that fills every cubic centimetre but costs a re-handle at every stop — and can't pass a roadside inspection — isn't a good plan. It's an expensive one.

Buried deliveries — unpack half the truck to reach stop 1 Illegal axle loads — under gross weight, still over an axle Unsafe under braking — nothing blocking the load forward

Who feels it

Stuck between a spreadsheet and a six-figure suite.

Small and mid-sized distributors and 3PLs running repeat multi-stop routes — vans, box trucks, curtain-siders. Too complex for hand-drawn sketches, too small for a heavyweight enterprise TMS.

Today

Manual & error-prone

Spreadsheets and gut feel. Bad master data, missed axle checks, surprises at the dock.

The gap

Nothing in the middle

Toy packers ignore delivery order and safety; enterprise suites are slow to buy, deploy and learn.

The want

Safe plans, in minutes

Right vehicle, right trips, a plan the warehouse can build and the driver can defend.

Why it wins

Three things most packers get wrong.

01

Delivery-order aware

Cargo loads back-to-front so stop 1 comes out first. No unpacking the truck at every drop — and it counts the extra moves when a layout forces them.

02

Door aware

Every box routes through a real door — rear or side — never through a wall. Slots that can't be reached past loaded cargo are rejected up front.

03

Physically honest

Axle & centre-of-gravity limits, steering-axle load, securing forces (EN 12195-1), re-checked after every stop. Labeled planning estimates — never fake certainty.

Delivery-order-aware packing and door-aware unloading are exactly the features the market says are missing — and the hardest to fake.

Under the hood

A deterministic engine you can trust and reproduce.

A greedy best-fit heuristic scores extreme-point candidates on compactness, axle balance, door access, delivery order and support — validating overlap, stacking and payload on every placement, committing only reachable slots.

It runs in a Web Worker with progress and cancel, off the main thread. A seeded RNG makes it fully reproducible: same seed → identical plan, every run. No Math.random in the domain. Export any scenario as JSON.

The honest twist

The AI is in how we built it. The product itself is deterministic math — no LLM in the loop, by design. In logistics, a plan you can reproduce and defend beats a plan you have to trust.

What's next

The engine is built. Next, we put it to work.

Three planned improvements — each one turns the deterministic core into something that advises, teaches and proves its own value.

01

You vs. the optimizer

Load the truck yourself in 3D, with live rule feedback on every placement — then watch LoadWise's plan and a side-by-side score: fewer trips, no buried stops, legal axles. The advantage of correct ordering, demonstrated, not asserted.

02

Explainable placements

03

Right-vehicle advice

From the packages to deliver, LoadWise recommends the vehicle type — van, box truck or curtain-sider — before a single box is placed. Right-size every route, don't pay for empty air.
